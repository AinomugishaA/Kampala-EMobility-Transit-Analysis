# Kampala E-Mobility Transition Study: Petrol vs. Electric

An end-to-end data analytics project evaluating the economic and environmental (climate) impacts of transitioning from internal combustion engine (ICE) petrol Boda Bodas to clean, hydro-powered electric motorcycles in Kampala, Uganda.

##  Core Data Insights
*   **85% Operating Cost Drop:** Electric motorcycles cost roughly **UGX 2,132** per day to operate compared to **UGX 15,349** for conventional petrol variants under baseline Kampala fuel pumps and domestic electricity tariffs.
*   **99% Carbon Footprint Mitigation:** Driven by Uganda's highly renewable, hydro-dominant national electricity grid, charging an EV motorcycle produces near-zero emissions (~42g CO₂ daily) compared to burning fossil fuels (~5,583g CO₂ daily).
*   **Economic Impact:** Choosing an electric motorcycle over petrol saves an average Kampala delivery/Boda rider over **UGX 66,000** within just a 5-day operational tracking loop.
